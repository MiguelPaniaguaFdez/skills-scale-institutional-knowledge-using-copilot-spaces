# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Scrum Master**: Participates in ceremonies, reports blockers
- **Product Managers**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Provides effort estimates and technical risk assessments
- **UX Designer**: Implements designs and provides implementation feasibility feedback
- **QA Lead**: Collaborates on test automation and testability
- **Technical Writer**: Reviews documentation for technical accuracy
- **DevOps Engineer**: Uses CI/CD pipelines, contributes to infrastructure code
- **Data Analyst**: Implements instrumentation and tracking

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Defines requirements and validates delivered features
- **Project Managers**: Collaborates on roadmap planning and prioritization
- **Scrum Master**: Partners on backlog refinement and sprint planning
- **UX Designer**: Collaborates on feature discovery and validates user research
- **QA Lead**: Defines acceptance criteria and validates feature quality
- **Technical Writer**: Aligns documentation with product roadmap
- **Data Analyst**: Defines success metrics and reviews feature impact

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on roadmap, scope, and priorities
- **Scrum Master**: Partners on dependency management and risk escalation
- **Developers**: Coordinates timelines and resource allocation
- **QA Lead**: Tracks testing progress and quality risks
- **DevOps Engineer**: Coordinates deployment schedules and infrastructure changes
- **Technical Writer**: Ensures documentation is complete for releases
- **Data Analyst**: Uses metrics to track project health
- **UX Designer**: Coordinates design review and approval milestones

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile practices, remove impediments, and coach teams on self-organization and cross-functionality. They ensure the team follows agreed processes and continuously improves.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, daily standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow team velocity
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external disruptions and context-switching
- Track and visualize team metrics (velocity, burndown, cycle time)
- Foster continuous improvement through retrospective action items
- Collaborate with Product Managers on backlog refinement
- Partner with Project Managers on dependency and risk management

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and delivery predictability
- Enable team self-organization and autonomy
- Drive continuous process improvements
- Maintain healthy team dynamics and morale

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standup meetings
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- Weekly coordination with Project Managers on blockers
- Metrics dashboards and velocity reports

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes blockers, facilitates ceremonies
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on backlog refinement and sprint planning
- **Project Managers**: Partners on dependency management, risk escalation, and status reporting
- **QA Lead**: Coordinates on Definition of Done and testing integration into sprints
- **DevOps Engineer**: Ensures deployment processes fit within sprint cadence

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive experiences, and validate solutions through testing. They bridge user research insights with product development to ensure usability and customer satisfaction.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research (interviews, surveys, usability tests)
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user flows and information architecture
- Establish and maintain design systems and style guides
- Validate designs through user testing and feedback
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature discovery
- Partner with Developers on implementation feasibility
- Conduct accessibility reviews and ensure WCAG compliance

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce friction in user journeys
- Validate design decisions through user research
- Maintain design consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and insights presentations
- Prototype walkthroughs and usability test reports
- Design specification handoffs with acceptance criteria

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on feature discovery, validates solutions with users
- **Developers**: Provides design specifications, reviews implementation for fidelity
- **Technical Writer**: Shares user research insights to inform documentation approach
- **Data Analyst**: Uses analytics to identify pain points and validate design improvements
- **QA Lead**: Defines acceptance criteria for visual and interaction quality

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate, and user-friendly documentation for products, APIs, and internal processes. They ensure information is accessible and maintainable for diverse audiences.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain product documentation (user guides, tutorials, release notes)
- Create API documentation and code examples
- Document internal processes and runbooks
- Establish documentation standards and style guides
- Review technical accuracy with subject matter experts
- Organize information architecture for discoverability
- Conduct documentation usability testing
- Keep documentation synchronized with product releases

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear self-service documentation
- Enable users and developers to be successful quickly
- Maintain up-to-date and accurate technical content
- Improve documentation discoverability and searchability

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Release notes and change announcements
- Documentation standards and style guide updates
- User feedback analysis and doc improvement plans

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Reviews technical accuracy, documents APIs and features
- **Product Managers**: Aligns documentation with product roadmap and feature releases
- **UX Designer**: Leverages user research for documentation approach
- **QA Lead**: Validates documentation matches actual product behavior
- **Data Analyst**: Uses metrics to identify documentation gaps

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts collect, analyze, and interpret data to inform product decisions, measure success metrics, and identify opportunities for improvement. They enable data-driven decision making across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Build dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Conduct A/B tests and statistical analysis
- Identify trends, patterns, and anomalies in data
- Partner with Product Managers on success metrics definition
- Create data models and maintain data quality
- Provide insights for prioritization and feature validation
- Document analysis methodologies and findings

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across teams
- Measure and prove impact of product changes
- Identify growth opportunities through data insights
- Ensure data quality and accessibility

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews with Product Managers
- Dashboard walkthroughs and insights presentations
- A/B test results and recommendations
- Data quality reports and anomaly alerts

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Defines success metrics, validates feature impact
- **Developers**: Specifies instrumentation requirements for tracking
- **UX Designer**: Provides analytics to identify user pain points
- **Project Managers**: Tracks delivery metrics and project health indicators
- **DevOps Engineer**: Collaborates on observability and monitoring

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define quality standards, test strategies, and oversee testing activities to ensure products meet acceptance criteria and quality goals. They advocate for quality throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategies and quality standards
- Create and maintain test plans and test cases
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Perform exploratory testing and edge case validation
- Review acceptance criteria for testability
- Manage bug triage and defect tracking
- Ensure testing coverage for critical user flows
- Champion quality practices and shift-left testing
- Coordinate with Developers on test automation
- Conduct release readiness assessments

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, reliable products to customers
- Prevent production defects through comprehensive testing
- Reduce time-to-detection for bugs
- Improve testability and test automation coverage

### Typical Communication
- Daily bug triage and priority discussions
- Test plan reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Release readiness reports and go/no-go recommendations
- Quality metrics dashboards (defect rates, coverage, test pass rates)

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test automation, reviews testability
- **Product Managers**: Validates acceptance criteria, assesses feature readiness
- **Scrum Master**: Integrates QA activities into sprint workflow
- **DevOps Engineer**: Coordinates on CI/CD test automation and staging environments
- **Project Managers**: Reports on quality risks and testing progress

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and monitoring systems that enable reliable, automated software delivery. They bridge development and operations for continuous deployment.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC) and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment and rollback procedures
- Ensure security scanning in build pipelines
- Maintain staging and production environments
- Optimize build times and deployment efficiency
- Conduct incident response and post-mortem analysis
- Partner with Developers on containerization and orchestration
- Implement disaster recovery and backup procedures

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and automated deployments
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents
- Improve observability and operational excellence

### Typical Communication
- Deployment status updates and release coordination
- Incident reports and post-mortem summaries
- Infrastructure change proposals and runbooks
- Performance and availability metrics dashboards

### Interaction Points with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides CI/CD tools, collaborates on containerization
- **QA Lead**: Maintains test environments, integrates automated tests in pipelines
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates deployment schedules and infrastructure changes
- **Scrum Master**: Ensures deployment processes fit sprint cadence
- **Data Analyst**: Collaborates on observability and system metrics
